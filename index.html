From Inspiration to Reality: Our Trial and Error Process

Script Evolution

In the early stages, we faced the problem of an overly complex narrative. After several discussions, we kept simplifying it, retaining only the core emotion.

[Replace with: Screenshot of the discarded first draft]

First Draft: Too Complicated

Too many characters and frequent scene changes. Considering the difficulty of the "cut-out" style stop-motion animation, we realized this was impossible to complete in 3 days.

[Replace with: Screenshot of the final script]

Final Draft: Pure Narration in a Fairy Tale Style

We removed all dialogues and changed it to a pure narration. This not only reduced our workload but also added a storybook feel to the animation.

Character and Prop Development

Making movable cut-out joints was our biggest technical challenge.

[Replace with: Image of the failed tape prop]

First Attempt: Transparent Tape Fixing

Initially, we used transparent tape to connect the character's arms. We found it very stiff, unable to show delicate expressions, and too small to film.

[Replace with: Image/GIF of the successful wire prop]

Final Solution: Larger Size + Wire Joints

After re-testing, we doubled the size of the characters and used fine wire through the joints, achieving smooth movement control.
